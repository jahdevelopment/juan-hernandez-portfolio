  • Password Management

    In the digital world, people usually have a huge number of services and the way to access those is through login to a personal account with certain personal information and a unique key or password; but, this system of user recognition is in the reality the weakest link of the chain of security due to regular users do not have a proper password system to maintain their accounts free of malicious intends.

    There are now multiple efforts to change the control access like biometrics mechanisms of fingerprints or facial recognition, but at the moment they are not easily accessible to all users, therefore we are still depending on passwords.

    Below, you will have some recommendations to improve your expertise in password management, no matter if you are an occasional user or a professional depending on any software interface to develop your daily tasks.

    • First of all, is important to know that a password never has to have personal names, dates of birth, pets' names, addresses, phone numbers, repeated characters, series of characters, e.g., 123456, or any other element easy to guess for others.

    • Design your passwords with more than 8 characters (in this case more is better), combining lowercase and uppercase letters, include as well more than one number and at least one special character, e.g., #,$,%,&,/,(,),=. Note: do not use < or > signs, both can cause problems with web browsers.

    • Try to not write your password in any digital file, nor write it down on paper, make the way to remember it forever.

    • Do not share the password with another person, always there are people or your entire trust, but you do not know if this person is aware of having good password management.

    • Change your password regularly, at least twice a year, or change it when you have a suspicious anyone knows it. Do not use the same password to access all services, be aware of using passwords in apps you do not know trust.

    • ever access important accounts, e.g., bank accounts, while you are connected in public wifi or you are using a lending device, like an internet cafe.

    • If the system allows, implement mechanisms to make stronger your access, like two-factor authentication, to maintain a stronger level of security to verify the entrance of your accounts.

    • This process depends on another factor like a token generator or another device to verify identities.

    • And last but not least advice indeed is the most important recommendation is the use of passwords manager applications, which enhances a better experience for users who have to remember a bunch of passwords.

    A password manager is a vault for storing all our access mechanisms (user and id) and controlling them with just one master password.

    Most of them have the option to auto-generate very strong and long passwords with the goal of not giving personal access keys to accounts that do not seem trustable.

    There are also multiple types of password manager services, some of them are free or included in your web browser, others are extensions of your antivirus software or VPN software, and others paid services can offer special features like multi-platform access, number of users, number of passwords they can store, and more.
